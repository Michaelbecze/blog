I'm Michael — a Network Engineer with over five years of hands-on experience in enterprise infrastructure. My focus is on routing and switching, BGP, overlay networks, and the kind of operational troubleshooting that only comes from time spent in the trenches. 

## What I Do

On any given day I might be replacing old hardware, tuning EIGRP, standing up a VXLAN/EVPN fabric, writing a Python script to automate a provisioning task, or digging into a Palo Alto firewall policy. I've delivered data center migrations and core infrastructure replacements with zero downtime, and I work directly with customers and vendors to track problems down to root cause rather than symptoms.

My platform experience spans Cisco IOS/IOS XE/NX-OS, Nexus 5K/7K/9K, Catalyst 9K series, ISR/ASR routers, Palo Alto PAN-OS, Fortinet FortiGate, and cloud connectivity on Azure and AWS.

## About This Blog

This is where I write about what I'm actively working on, studying, and figuring out. Some posts are detailed technical write-ups, others are lab notes or half-formed ideas still in progress — but all of it comes from real experience, not documentation rewrites.

If you're interested in enterprise networking, routing protocols, or the details that live a few layers below the surface, you're in the right place.
